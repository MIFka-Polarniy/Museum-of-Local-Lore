Leonid Vitalievich Sobinov
Audio guide decoding
Leonid Vitalievich Sobinov, Russian opera singer, lyric tenor, was born on 7 June 1872 in Yaroslavl.
In 1881 the boy entered the gymnasium. In the gymnasium choir he began to learn singing. In 1888 his first successful performance took place. In 1890 Leonid Sobinov graduated from the gymnasium with a silver medal. In autumn 1892 he entered the Music and Drama School. In 1897 he became a soloist of the Bolshoi Theatre, toured extensively. He performed in Monte Carlo, Madrid, London, Berlin, Paris and travelled a lot thoughaut. He left the stage at the age of 60 in 1933.
How connected with the Lyutovsky region: Leonid Vitalievich's grandfather Vasily was a sert peasant. He earned money in the waste trades and bought himself off. He built a house 'sobinno', isolated', i.e. away from the village of Torgovtsevo. By the way, this is one of the versions of the origin of the singer's surname.
Leonid Vitalievich was in the village of Torgovtsevo twice: the first time at the funeral of his grandfather Vasily, and the second time when he made out the inheritance. The house has not survived to this day.
