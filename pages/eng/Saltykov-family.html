The Saltykov family
Audio guide decoding
It was established that the estate in the village of Nikolskoye belonged to the noble family of Saltykovs. The Saltykovs are an ancient Moscow boyar family, known since the middle of the 14th century. It is one of the most noble Russian families. They played an outstanding role in the history of Russia, occupied prominent military and civil posts, were close to the tsar's and then the imperial court.
Pyotr Semyonovich Saltykov - Count, Field Marshal-General, Senator and Cavalier. He was born in 1698, began his service as a soldier of Peter the Great's guard in 1714. Two decades he lived in France, studied marine science. Returning to Russia, he served in the Guards and at court. During the Seven Years' War (1756-1762) he became commander-in-chief of the Russian army. His estate in Yaroslavl uyezd included the village of Nikolskoye, the villages of Kogayevo, Timoshino, Golenishchevo, Podolino, Telishchevo, Ananyino, Sergeyevo, Cherneevo and Timokhino.
